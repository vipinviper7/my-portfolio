Recent Updates — Zoho Sprints

Designing a centralized in-product updates system for Zoho Sprints

Product: Zoho Sprints
Role: Visual Designer
Timeline: ~2 weeks
Status: Shipped

The Brief

Zoho Sprints had no way to talk to its users from inside the product. Product updates, subscription reminders, community posts, promotions — none of it reached users in-app.

My manager handed me a requirements doc and a clear ask: design an updates system that handles all of this without overwhelming people.

What Made It Tricky

The challenge wasn't "design a notification panel." It was fitting very different types of content into one space.

Product announcements users need to know about. Subscription reminders that require action. Community updates that are nice-to-have. Promotions most people ignore. All competing for the same screen space, all arriving at different frequencies.

Show too much, users ignore everything. Show too little, they miss what matters.

How the Design Evolved

~10 iterations over two weeks. Here's how my thinking shifted:

Early on — I jumped into designing structured cards with clear hierarchy. Looked polished, but feedback was it's overdesigned. Too heavy for what this needed to be.

The key shift — My manager pushed toward simplicity, and it was the right call. These needed to behave like notifications — scannable, lightweight — but carry more information than a typical notification. That tension drove the rest of the process.

Where I drifted — A few iterations went off-script from Sprints' existing design language. Looked good in isolation, felt wrong in the product. Pulling back to align with what existed actually made decisions easier.

What landed — Notification-style items with clear titles and enough room for context underneath. Simple enough to scan, detailed enough to be useful.

Final Design

A single update feed inside the product. One place for everything.

  • One stream, no tabs. Everything sorted by time.
  • Visual distinction by update type — users can tell at a glance what needs attention.
  • Scannable by default, expandable for detail.
  • Action-driven updates (like subscription reminders) visually separated from passive ones.

After Shipping

The core design shipped intact. The icons were redrawn during development to better match Sprints' existing icon style — flagged during review.

Before this feature, there was no in-product communication channel. Now there is.

What I Took Away

Simpler was harder. My instinct was to design something rich. Simplifying felt like a step backward — but it made the design better.

Stay inside the design language. The iterations where I went off-script looked interesting but felt disconnected. The best version felt like it had always been there.

Fast iteration beats perfect first drafts. Ten iterations in two weeks. None of them were precious. That helped.
