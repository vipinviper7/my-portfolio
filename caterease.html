CaterEase

Discover trusted caterers, book with confidence, track every detail

Product: CaterEase (Catering Discovery & Booking App)
Role: UX Researcher, Designer & Developer
Program: ABNUX Design Program
Status: Research complete, App shipped
Tools used: Figma, Claude Code, React Native (Expo), Supabase, NativeWind

CaterEase is a mobile app for discovering catering providers and booking catering services, designed for urban Indian families and professionals in Tier-1 cities. This project went from end-to-end UX research (stakeholder interviews, user interviews, personas, journey maps) through to a fully functional React Native app with 26 screens, 12 demo caterers, and 114 menu items.

The Problem

Booking a caterer in India is a fragmented, anxiety-filled experience. Users rely on word-of-mouth, phone calls, and WhatsApp to find, compare, and book caterers. The process has no transparency, no accountability, and no digital infrastructure.

  • Hidden charges — delivery, setup, and staff costs are revealed after booking
  • No written agreements — caterers change menu items without notice
  • Zero visibility — no way to track preparation or delivery on event day
  • No accountability — bad experiences go unreported with no verified review system
  • Portion confusion — users don't know how much food to order for their guest count

Research

The research began with a stakeholder interview (20 questions) with a catering aggregator product owner, then moved to 8 semi-structured user interviews across different economic segments in Chennai, covering events from birthdays and weddings to corporate lunches and poojas.

Key research findings:

  • Most users call 3–5 caterers before deciding — there's no way to compare side-by-side
  • Price per plate varies wildly with no transparency — same caterer gives different prices to different customers
  • Trust is the #1 factor — users want FSSAI verification, real photos, and verified reviews
  • Communication is fragmented across phone calls and WhatsApp with no single source of truth
  • Budget-conscious users prioritize price, corporate users need reliability and invoicing, quality-seekers want customization and accountability

Personas

Three personas emerged from the research, each representing a distinct segment of catering customers:

Arun — Budget-Conscious Parent
Age 38 · Govt. employee · ₹5–7 LPA · Birthdays, family functions, poojas
  • Wants affordable catering within ₹120–200 per plate with no surprise charges
  • Calls 4–5 caterers and negotiates heavily, decides based on word-of-mouth and price
  • Frustrated by hidden delivery/setup charges and no written proof of agreed menu
"I just want to know the full cost upfront. Every time there's some extra charge I didn't expect."
Priya — Corporate Event Admin
Age 30 · IT Admin Manager · ₹8–12 LPA · Corporate lunches, team events, client meetings
  • Needs to book quickly with minimal back-and-forth and get proper GST invoices
  • Searches online, wants to compare 3+ options side-by-side with quick turnaround
  • Food arrived 30 minutes late to a client meeting — no tracking was available
"I need something I can book in 10 minutes and trust that it'll show up on time."
Abishek — Quality-Seeking Professional
Age 34 · Software Engineer · ₹15–20 LPA · Weddings, anniversaries, housewarming
  • Researches extensively, reads every review, willing to pay premium for quality and transparency
  • Can't find reliable pure vegetarian caterers with detailed menus and real photos
  • Paid ₹40,000 advance but received mediocre quality with no recourse or accountability
"I paid premium but got average food. There should be a way to hold caterers accountable."

User Journey

The user journey spans 5 stages — Pre-Search, Search & Compare, Booking & Payment, Event Day, and Post-Event. Each stage was mapped across all three personas to identify design opportunities:

  • Pre-Search: No centralized source → Smart recommendations + cuisine/dietary filters
  • Search & Compare: No standard pricing → Standardized caterer cards with rating, price, verified badge
  • Booking & Payment: Hidden charges, no agreement → Full cost breakdown + digital contract with locked menu
  • Event Day: No visibility → 7-stage order tracking timeline with push notifications
  • Post-Event: No feedback channel → Verified review system with category ratings

Home & Discovery

The home screen provides multiple entry points: quick filter chips (Pure Veg, Wedding, Under ₹500, Top Rated), a featured caterers carousel, cuisine category grid, and a full search with filters for cuisine, event type, budget range, rating, dietary preferences, and guest count.

Each caterer card shows the key decision-making info at a glance: cover photo, name, verified badge, rating, cuisine tags, and price range — directly addressing the "can't compare" pain point.

Booking Flow

The booking flow is a 4-step pipeline designed for maximum transparency:

  • Step 1 — Event Details: Event type selector (6 types), date/time pickers, guest count stepper with caterer min/max, venue address
  • Step 2 — Menu Selection: Category tabs, per-item selection with veg/non-veg indicators, running price-per-plate counter, link to Portion Planner
  • Step 3 — Order Summary: Locked menu, complete price breakdown (food + staff + setup + delivery + GST), payment schedule (30% advance / 70% balance), digital agreement checkbox
  • Step 4 — Confirmation: Booking ID, summary, and action buttons

The order summary directly solves Arun's #1 frustration: no more hidden charges.

Order Tracking

A 7-stage vertical timeline tracks every booking from confirmation to completion:

  • Booking Confirmed → Menu Locked → Advance Paid → In Preparation → Event Day → Service Complete → Review Submitted

This directly addresses Priya's pain point — "food arrived 30 minutes late with no tracking." Each stage shows a timestamp and status, with direct access to message the caterer or view the locked menu.

Key Features

  • In-app messaging — Direct chat with caterers, quick reply chips, simulated auto-responses
  • Verified reviews — Post-event ratings across 4 categories (taste, service, value, cleanliness) tied to actual bookings — addressing Abishek's accountability concern
  • Portion Planner — Course-wise quantity recommendations based on guest count
  • Saved Addresses — CRUD with labels, default selection for quick rebooking
  • Referral Program — "Give ₹500, Get ₹500" with shareable referral codes

AARRR Metrics

  • Acquisition: SEO-optimized listings, sharable caterer profiles, referral invite links
  • Activation: Frictionless phone OTP onboarding, instant search with smart filters
  • Retention: Booking history with one-tap rebooking, saved favorites, portion planner tool
  • Revenue: 10–15% commission per booking, premium caterer listings, transparent in-app payments
  • Referral: Post-event share prompts, "Give ₹500, Get ₹500" program, referral tracking dashboard

Technical Implementation

The research and design were brought to life as a fully functional React Native app, vibecoded with Claude Code:

  • React Native (Expo SDK 54) with expo-router for file-based navigation
  • NativeWind v4 (Tailwind CSS) for the warm coral (#E86A50) design system
  • Zustand for state management + TanStack Query for server state
  • Supabase backend (Postgres + Auth + Realtime + Storage) with full schema — 12 tables, RLS policies, triggers
  • Plus Jakarta Sans typography across 4 weights
  • 57 source files across 26 screens, 11 UI components, 6 hooks, 3 stores
  • Demo mode with 12 caterers, 114 menu items, and 18 reviews — fully functional without a backend
