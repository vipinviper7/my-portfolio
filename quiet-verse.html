Quiet Verse

I wanted a Bible app that felt like opening a book, not opening an app store. So I built one.

Role: Designer & Vibecoder
Status: Shipped
Built with: Claude Code, React, Tailwind CSS, Capacitor

Why This Exists

I read scripture most mornings. It's a quiet part of my day — just me, coffee, and a verse to sit with.

But every Bible app I tried kept pulling me out of that moment. Notifications. Social features. “Share this verse!” prompts. Daily streak badges. Ads between chapters.

It felt like these apps were designed for engagement metrics, not for someone trying to reflect. I wanted something that got out of the way — something that felt like holding a beautifully typeset book.

The Problem with Bible Apps

  • Cluttered interfaces — tabs, sidebars, banners, and pop-ups competing for attention
  • Social pressure — streaks, sharing prompts, and community features that distract from reading
  • Internet dependency — many apps need a connection to load scripture, which defeats the purpose of morning quiet time

The goal was simple: distraction-free, offline-first, beautiful.

Design Philosophy

Every decision was guided by one question: does this help or distract from reading?

Cream & Gold palette
Scripture should feel like a physical book — warm, paper-like tones that feel timeless rather than digital.
Dual typography
Inter for clean UI navigation, Cormorant Garamond for scripture text. The words of the Bible deserve an elegant serif.
Minimal chrome
No tabs, no sidebars. Just the text and subtle navigation. If you're reading Psalms, you should see Psalms — not a toolbar.
Dark mode
Deep navy with warm gold accents for nighttime reading. Not harsh black-and-white — something gentle on the eyes.

The App

  • Complete KJV Bible — 66 books, 31,102 verses, all offline
  • Daily verse on the home screen with save, shuffle, and jump-to-chapter
  • Verse bookmarking with haptic feedback
  • Adjustable font size for readability
  • Reflection timer with breathing cues
  • Smooth light/dark mode toggle

Built in 3 Hours

~3 hrs
From idea to working iOS app

This app was built entirely through conversation with Claude Code — no manual coding.

I described the vision and aesthetic in plain English. Claude generated the React components, styling, and data architecture. I iterated on feel and interactions through conversational feedback. Then I wrapped it as a native app with Capacitor in the same session.

The entire process — from “I want a calm Bible app” to a working iOS app with all 66 books loaded — took about three hours.

Under the hood:

  • React 19 with Tailwind CSS for theming
  • Capacitor for native iOS/Android shell
  • Complete offline operation — self-hosted fonts, localStorage for bookmarks
  • Lazy loading — each Bible book loads only when opened (114 KB initial load)

What I Learned

Constraints make better products. By deciding early that this app would have no social features, no accounts, and no internet requirement, every design decision became simpler. The constraint wasn't limiting — it was freeing.

Vibecoding is a real design tool. Describing what I wanted in natural language and iterating through conversation gave me a working product in hours, not weeks. It changed how I think about prototyping.

Sometimes the best feature is what you leave out. Every Bible app I tried had more features than Quiet Verse. That's the point.
